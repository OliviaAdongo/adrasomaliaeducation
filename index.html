Strengthening Education And Training In Somaila (SETS)

Impact Stories from EU supported Education Project in Somalia Delivered by Adventist Development Relief Agency (ADRA)

Education is a fundamental pillar for socio-economic development and stability, particularly in fragile and conflict-affected contexts such as Somalia . Decades of civil unrest, political instability, and underinvestment in education have significantly weakened the country’s education system, leaving millions of children and youth without access to quality learning opportunities . Despite various interventions by the government and development partners, systemic challenges persist, including inadequate infrastructure, insufficient teacher training, and a lack of standardized curriculum and assessment frameworks.

Adventist Development Relief Agency (ADRA) has been at the center of these initiatives, partnering with the Ministry of Education Culture and Higher Education (MOECHE) and other consortium members such as World Vision (WV), Relief International (RI), American Refugee Committee (ARC), and Comitato Internazionale per lo Sviluppo dei Popoli (CISP) to support the provision of quality education.

By addressing critical gaps in infrastructure, teacher training, and curriculum development, the SETS Project aims to lay the foundation for a brighter educational future in Somalia.

The SETS Project is dedicated to improving access to quality education for Somali children and youth, despite the ongoing challenges of conflict and instability

Notably, the SETS Project covered the South-central states/regions namely; Galmudug, Hir-Shabelle, South West and Jubaland, and Banadir of Somalia.

The SETS II Project emphasizes the provision of education training opportunities for girls and women, equipping them with knowledge, skills, capabilities, and rights to enjoy life in dignity and engage fully in society as responsible and productive adults, contributing to the social, economic, and environmental well-being of their communities.
