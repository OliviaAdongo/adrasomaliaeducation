Strengthening Education And Training In Somaila (SETS)

Impact Stories from EU supported Education Project in Somalia Delivered by Adventist Development Relief Agency (ADRA)

Education is a fundamental pillar for socio-economic development and stability, particularly in fragile and conflict-affected contexts such as Somalia. Years of civil unrest, political turmoil, and chronic underinvestment have deeply eroded the country’s education sector, leaving millions of children and youth without access to quality learning opportunities. While both the government and development partners have made efforts to revitalize the sector, major systemic issues persist. Challenges such as inadequate infrastructure, limited teacher capacity, and the absence of standardized curricula and assessment systems continue to undermine educational progress and deepen regional disparities. Addressing these barriers is essential for building an inclusive and resilient education system that can drive Somalia’s sustainable development.

Although there have been various interventions by the government and development partners, systemic challenges in Somalia’s education sector persist. Key issues include inadequate infrastructure, insufficient teacher training, and the absence of standardized curriculum and assessment frameworks. These gaps continue to hinder the delivery of quality education, limit student learning outcomes, and exacerbate regional disparities, particularly in rural and marginalized areas. Addressing these systemic challenges is essential for building a more resilient, inclusive, and effective education system that can support Somalia’s long-term development goals.

Adventist Development Relief Agency (ADRA) has been at the center of these initiatives, partnering with theMinistry of Education, Culture, and Higher Education (MOECHE) and other consortium members such as World Vision (WV), Relief International (RI), the American Refugee Committee (ARC), and Comitato Internazionale per lo Sviluppo dei Popoli (CISP) to support the provision of quality education.

By addressing critical gaps in infrastructure, teacher training, and curriculum development, the SETS Project aims to lay the foundation for a brighter educational future in Somalia.

The SETS Project is dedicated to improving access to quality education for Somali children and youth, despite the ongoing challenges of conflict and instability.

Strengthening Education and Training in Somalia (SETS) Project partnered with key organizations for educational improvements.
Notably, the SETS Project was implemented across the South-Central regions of Somalia, covering Galmudug, Hir-Shabelle, South West, Jubaland, and Banadir. By focusing on these regions, the project aimed to address the significant educational disparities caused by years of conflict and underinvestment.
These areas, often characterized by fragile governance and limited access to basic services, were prioritized to ensure that the most vulnerable populations could benefit from improved educational opportunities and capacity-building initiatives.

The SETS II Project emphasizes the provision of education training opportunities for girls and women, equipping them with knowledge, skills, capabilities, and rights to enjoy life with dignity and engage fully in society as responsible and productive adults, contributing to the social, economic, and environmental well-being of their communities.
